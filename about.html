Professional Chess Association of the Philippines

PROFESSIONAL CHESS ASSOCIATION OF THE PHILIPPINES

Professionals

PCAP was formed, 45 years after PBA with the vision to give millions of Filipino Chess enthusiasts the opportunity to showcase their skill and love of the game.

On July 28, 2020, The Games and Amusement Board (GAB) adopted Resolution 2020-02, approving and establishing the Professional Chess Association of the Philippines (PCAP), the very first professional chess league in the Philippines and in the world.

Thus, PCAP was formed, forty five (45) years after the Philippine Basketball Association (PBA) was established. At 10 am of September 17, 2020 a virtual joint press conference was held by GAB & PCAP to announce the founding of the PCAP.

Soon thereafter, twenty (20) commercial chess clubs from all over the country committed to become part of league, with several more clubs finalizing their intent to participate.

MISSION

Mission To give Filipino chess enthusiasts the opportunity to hone and showcase their skills, and promote the love of the game – by ensuring integrity and fairness for professional chess in the Philippines.

History Of Chess In The Philippines

The history of Chess in the Philippines should be remembered by all chess-loving Filipinos. In 1978, the Philippines became the center of the chess world, when the World Chess Championship between Anatoly Karpov and Challenger Victor Korchnoi was held in Baguio City. It turned out to be one of the most celebrated matches in chess history.

Furthermore, Hon. Florencio Campomanes brought the Philippines on the world map and in many international publications when the big names of the sports came to the Philippine shores in the early ‘70s, with the Marlboro Chess Classics.

He also became the Philippine National Champion in 1956 and 1960 also represented the country at five Chess Olympiads.
